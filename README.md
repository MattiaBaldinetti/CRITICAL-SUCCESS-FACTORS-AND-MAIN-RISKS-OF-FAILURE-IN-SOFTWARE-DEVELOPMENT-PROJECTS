# CRITICAL-SUCCESS-FACTORS-AND-MAIN-RISKS-OF-FAILURE-IN-SOFTWARE-DEVELOPMENT-PROJECTS
This paper explores the critical success and failure factors in software development projects, aiming to compare the well-established findings in the literature with the current perceptions and experiences of industry professionals. Through a systematic analysis of academic studies and official articles from recent decades, the main people- and process-related variables influencing project outcomes are identified. Afterwards, a targeted questionnaire was distributed to collect empirical data from the IT community, in order to assess any changes in the priorities given to critical factors compared to what emerges from the literature. The comparative analysis of the results highlights similarities and differences in the importance of traditionally relevant factors and the emergence of new elements not yet structured in previous studies. Finally, possible future developments and practical implications for the management and optimization of software development processes in constantly evolving competitive environments are discussed.
